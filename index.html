MILO: Voice-Controlled Robotic Arm

A robotic arm that responds to a user's voice commands to assist paraplegic patients & patients with limited upper limb strength to do daily tasks, such as connect with family via video calling & drink water. My primary contribution is voice recognition & response system by building a microphone that is able to hear the user in noisy environments, optimizing the system for lower costs and a smaller size while maintaining high audio quality, and integrating the system with Arduino's speech recognition. This project made it to RESNA Student Design Challenge Finalist 2024 and recceived an honorable mention from the TOM Global Innovation Challenge 2025.
